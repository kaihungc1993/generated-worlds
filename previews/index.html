The portfolio as a card game

Every asset, sim scene and generated world becomes a collectible card — asset cards summon their 3D model out of the card, scene cards pull you into the world with a camera dive. Two previews answer one question: should everything live in one deck, or in a deck per category?

1

One Deck

A single merged deck riffle-shuffles and fans into a browsable hand. Category chips re-deal the fan with animation — filtering, not navigating. Best if quick cross-category browsing matters most.

4

Four Decks

A dealer's card table with four face-down decks, one per category (the game deck is a "coming attraction" teaser). Pick a deck and it deals into a fan; gather sweeps it back. Best if categories should feel like distinct rooms.

☾

Tarot Draw

A mystical bonus take: the deck swirls, deals three cards of fate face-down, and each flips with an arcane glow — a serendipity mode that surfaces random highlights rather than letting you browse.

Category Lanes

Streaming-service browse: four colored lanes, one per category, drag-with-momentum rows with tilt + holo shine and lane parallax. Scales to any card count; the four types are unmissable.

◎

Orbit Wheel

All ~30 cards on one segmented 3D ring — drag to spin with momentum, a colored dial + legend jumps between category arcs, and the front card enlarges with its story. Browsing is spin → read → click.
open →
▦

Command Grid

AAA character-select: compact tile grid on the left (all cards visible, grouped by category), live stage on the right — assets rotate in real 3D as you hover, scenes drift with Ken Burns. Enter summons or dives.
